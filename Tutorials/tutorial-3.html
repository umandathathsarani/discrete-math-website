Question 1: Sets vs. Sequences

Name two primary differences between a Set and a Sequence.

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
1. In a sequence, order matters (it is an ordered list).
2. In a sequence, duplicates are allowed (e.g., 1, 1, 2, 3...).

Question 2: Notation

If you see the notation {an} in discrete mathematics, what does it represent?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
It represents the entire sequence. (Whereas an without the braces refers to a single specific term in the sequence).

Question 3: Generating a Sequence

Find the first four terms of the sequence given by the formula an = 3n.

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
Since sequences generally index from n = 1:
a1 = 3(1) = 3
a2 = 3(2) = 6
a3 = 3(3) = 9
a4 = 3(4) = 12
The sequence is: 3, 6, 9, 12...

Question 4: Arithmetic Sequence Definition

What defines an Arithmetic Sequence?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
An arithmetic sequence increases or decreases by a constant amount (addition or subtraction), known as the common difference (d).

Question 5: Finding the Common Difference

What is the common difference (d) of the arithmetic sequence: 7, 3, -1, -5...?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
d = Term 2 - Term 1
d = 3 - 7 = -4

Question 6: Arithmetic nth Term

What is the formula to find the nth term (Tn) of an Arithmetic Progression?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
Tn = a + (n - 1)d

Question 7: Arithmetic Calculation

Find the 10th term of the arithmetic sequence: 5, 8, 11, 14...

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
a = 5, d = 3, n = 10
T10 = 5 + (10 - 1)3
T10 = 5 + (9)(3)
T10 = 5 + 27 = 32

Question 8: Geometric Sequence Definition

What defines a Geometric Sequence?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
A geometric sequence increases or decreases by a constant factor (multiplication or division), known as the common ratio (r).

Question 9: Finding the Common Ratio

What is the common ratio (r) of the geometric sequence: 2, 10, 50, 250...?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
r = Term 2 / Term 1
r = 10 / 2 = 5

Question 10: Geometric nth Term

What is the formula to find the nth term (un) of a Geometric Progression?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
un = arn - 1

Question 11: Geometric Calculation

Find the 6th term of the sequence given by bn = 2n.

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
b6 = 26
b6 = 64

Question 12: Arithmetic Sum Formula

What are the two formulas used to find the sum (Sn) of an Arithmetic sequence?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
1. Sn = (n/2) [2a + (n - 1)d]
2. Sn = (n/2) [a + l] (where l is the last term)

Question 13: Geometric Sum Formula

Write the formula for the sum (Sn) of a Geometric sequence where the ratio r is greater than 1.

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
Sn = a(rn - 1) / (r - 1)

Question 14: Expanding Sigma Notation I

Expand and solve: Σ(i=0 to 3) i

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
0 + 1 + 2 + 3 = 6

Question 15: Expanding Sigma Notation II

Expand and solve: Σ(i=1 to 4) (i2 + 3)

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
= (1² + 3) + (2² + 3) + (3² + 3) + (4² + 3)
= (4) + (7) + (12) + (19)
= 42

Question 16: Expanding Sigma Notation III

Expand and solve: Σ(i=1 to 3) 4

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
Because there is no variable 'i' to plug numbers into, you just repeat the constant 4 for each step (1, 2, and 3):
= 4 + 4 + 4 = 12

Question 17: Factorial Definition

How is n! mathematically defined?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
It is the product of all whole numbers from n down to 1.
n! = n(n-1)(n-2)...(2)(1)

Question 18: Special Factorial Rule

What is the value of 0! ?

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
1. (This is a special mathematical definition).

Question 19: Calculating Basic Factorials

Calculate the exact values for 5! and 7!.

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
5! = 5 * 4 * 3 * 2 * 1 = 120
7! = 7 * 6 * 5 * 4 * 3 * 2 * 1 = 5040

Question 20: Simplifying Factorial Algebra

Simplify the algebraic expression: n! / (n-2)!

🪄 Lumos! Reveal Answer

Answer:
Expand the top part until it matches the bottom part:
[n * (n-1) * (n-2)!] / (n-2)!
The (n-2)! terms cancel out, leaving:
n(n - 1) or n² - n
