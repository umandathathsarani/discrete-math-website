Lecture 8: Binary Trees, Heaps, and Spanning Trees

1. Tree Anatomy

A connected undirected graph with no simple circuits. A Rooted Tree has one designated top vertex.

  • Binary Tree: Every internal node has at most two children.
  • Full vs. Complete: A Full tree has exactly 0 or 2 children per node. A Complete tree is filled left-to-right on the bottom level.

2. Heaps & Prim's Algorithm

Max/Min Heap: A complete binary tree where the parent node is strictly greater than (or less than) its children.

Prim's Algorithm: A greedy algorithm to find a Minimum Spanning Tree. It repeatedly adds the lowest-weight edge that connects a vertex inside the tree to a vertex outside.
