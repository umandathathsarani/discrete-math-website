1. Introduction to Sequences

A Sequence is an ordered list of elements. It is similar to a Set, but with two major differences:

  • Duplicates are allowed: (e.g., 1, 1, 2, 3, 5...)
  • Order matters: The position of the element is strictly defined.

Notation:

  • an represents a specific term in the sequence.
  • {an} means the entire sequence (note: it uses the same curly bracket notation as sets!).
  • Sequences are generally indexed starting from 1 (i.e., a1, a2, a3...).

Examples:
If an = 3n, the sequence is 3, 6, 9, 12...
If bn = 2n, the sequence is 2, 4, 8, 16, 32...

2. Arithmetic Sequences

An arithmetic sequence (or progression) increases or decreases by a constant amount (called the common difference, d).

  • General Form: a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d...
  • The nth Term Formula: Tn = a + (n - 1)d
  • Sum of the first n terms (Sn):
    Sn = n⁄2 [2a + (n - 1)d]
    OR
    Sn = n⁄2 [a + l] (where l is the last term)
🪄 Wizard's Guide to Variables:
a = The first term
d = The common difference (Term 2 - Term 1)
n = The position number of the term

3. Geometric Sequences

A geometric sequence (or G.P.) increases or decreases by a constant factor. Each number is multiplied by the previous number by a common ratio (r).

  • General Form: a, ar, ar2, ar3...
  • The nth Term Formula: un = arn - 1
  • Sum of the first n terms (Sn):
    If r < 1: Sn = a(1 - rn) ⁄ (1 - r)
    If r > 1: Sn = a(rn - 1) ⁄ (r - 1)

4. Summations (Σ)

Sigma Notation is a concise mathematical way to represent the sum of a sequence of numbers.

Σ(j=m to n) aj = am + am+1 + am+2 + ... + an

🪄 Expansion Examples

  • a) Σ(i=0 to 3) i = 0 + 1 + 2 + 3 = 6
  • b) Σ(i=1 to 4) (i2 + 3) = (12+3) + (22+3) + (32+3) + (42+3)
    = 4 + 7 + 12 + 19 = 42
  • c) Σ(i=1 to 3) 4 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12 (Since there is no 'i' in the formula, you just add the constant 4 three times).

5. Factorials (!)

The factorial of a number is the product of all the whole numbers from that number down to 1.

Formula: n! = n(n-1)(n-2)...(2)(1)

🪄 Special Case Rule: By mathematical definition, 0! = 1.

🪄 Calculation Examples

  • 5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120
  • 7! = 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 5040
  • n! / (n-2)! = [n(n-1)(n-2)!] / (n-2)! = n(n-1) or n2 - n
