Lecture 5: Combinations & Binomial Theorem

1. Combinations

An arrangement where order does NOT matter.

C(n, r) = n! / (r! × (n-r)!)

With Repetition: Choosing r items from n types allows C(n+r-1, r) ways.

2. Binomial Theorem & Pascal's Triangle

The theorem provides a way to expand expressions of the form (x+y)n.

Pascal's Identity: C(n, k) = C(n-1, k-1) + C(n-1, k). This forms Pascal's triangle, where each number is the sum of the two directly above it.
